# The Meridian Project — Goals (v2)

## Mission

To publicly present the most credible, verified-unidentified cases of anomalous aerial phenomena — each one deeply analyzed by AI, enriched with source documents, and connected to patterns across the full evidence record. Meridian exists to make the strongest evidence accessible to everyone, from the casually curious to the serious researcher.

## Core Belief

There is a body of officially unidentified cases — verified by military investigations, government programs, and credentialed witnesses — that deserves serious, public, analytical attention. The problem is not a lack of evidence. It is that the credible evidence is buried in archives, scattered across agencies, and drowned out by noise. Meridian filters the noise and presents what remains.

## What Success Looks Like

- A curated collection of verified-unidentified cases that anyone can browse, understand, and take seriously — without needing subject matter expertise
- AI analysis that surfaces the patterns connecting these cases: shared physical descriptors, geographic clusters, temporal correlations, and recurring witness experiences
- A homepage that tells the story of what the evidence shows, making the connections visible and compelling
- A case-detail experience deep enough to satisfy a journalist or academic, accessible enough for someone who just watched a congressional hearing and wants to know more
- Honest reporting: when the evidence doesn't support a conclusion, Meridian says so with the same rigor as a positive finding

## What Meridian Is

- A curated evidence platform. Every case has been verified as officially unidentified — explained sightings and likely hoaxes are excluded.
- A pattern-finding engine. AI connects cases across sources, decades, and geographies to surface what individual researchers cannot.
- An accessible front door. The homepage shows what the cases have in common. The case pages tell each story. You don't need to be an expert to get value.
- A living record. New cases and source documents are added as they become available, and AI analysis is re-run as the corpus grows.

## What Meridian Is Not

- A database of every UFO report ever filed. We don't include the full 150,000+ civilian reports — only the cases that have been verified as genuinely unidentified.
- A conspiracy research platform. Cover-up theories are out of scope.
- A fan community. The tone and methodology are those of a research institution.
- An advocacy project. Meridian does not start with a conclusion.

## The Central Question

**What does the verified evidence actually show?**

This is the single question the entire platform is organized around. The homepage answers it at a high level (patterns, connections, the big picture). Each case page answers it at the individual level (what happened, what evidence exists, how it connects). The query interface lets anyone ask their own version of the question.

## Guiding Principles

**Curation is the product.** Meridian's value is not volume — it's that we've done the work of filtering, verifying, and analyzing so the user doesn't have to.

**Cases are stories.** Every case is a narrative with context, evidence, and connections — not a row in a database. The case page is the heart of the platform.

**Show the work.** Every AI-extracted descriptor is labeled as AI-extracted. Every finding includes its methodology. Every score shows its factor breakdown.

**The homepage is the connections layer.** Individual cases are interesting. The patterns that connect them are extraordinary. The homepage makes those patterns visible.

**Null results matter.** If the data does not support a conclusion, that is a finding worth presenting. Meridian publishes negative results with the same design weight as positive ones.

**Accessible first, rigorous always.** The front door is designed for the curious public. The depth is there for researchers. Same platform, different levels of engagement.
