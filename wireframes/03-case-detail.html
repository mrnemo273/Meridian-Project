USS Nimitz Encounter (Tic Tac)

Date: November 14, 2004 Location: 31.5°N, 117.5°W — Off San Diego, CA Duration: ~5 minutes Witnesses: 6+
Sources: Pentagon AATIP Release · USS Princeton Radar Log · Cmdr. Fravor Testimony (Congress, 2023)
94
Tier 1
Case Narrative

On November 14, 2004, the USS Princeton, part of the Nimitz Carrier Strike Group, had been tracking anomalous aerial contacts on the AN/SPY-1B radar system for approximately two weeks. The objects appeared at altitudes above 80,000 feet before rapidly descending to hover near sea level.

Commander David Fravor and Lt. Commander Alex Dietrich were vectored to investigate a contact during a training exercise. Upon arrival at the coordinates, Fravor observed a white, oblong object — described as resembling a "Tic Tac" — approximately 40 feet in length, hovering and making erratic movements above a disturbance in the ocean surface.

When Fravor descended to engage, the object accelerated beyond visual range in less than two seconds. A second F/A-18 subsequently captured the object on FLIR (Forward Looking Infrared), producing the now-declassified "FLIR1" video.
