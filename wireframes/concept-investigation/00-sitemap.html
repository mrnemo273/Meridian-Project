Concept · Sitemap & Structure

Meridian: The Investigation

A human + AI cold case investigation. We reopen the 821 most credible UFO/UAP case files — reviewing evidence, building case boards, constructing timelines, and publishing our findings. Think X-Files meets Serial meets an FBI evidence wall.

The Concept

The old idea: A database of 821 cases you can browse. The problem: who cares? There are already repos and wikis. Reading raw case files isn't interesting.

The new idea: An ongoing investigation. Nemo (human researcher) and Claude (AI analyst) reopen each case together, building visual case boards, timelines, and evidence maps — then publishing findings. Each case is an "episode" with a narrative arc: what we know, what doesn't add up, what the AI found that nobody's connected before.

What makes it different: You're not browsing data — you're following a live investigation. The case boards, connection maps, and AI analysis are the product. The process IS the content.

Key Visual / UX Concepts

The Case Board — The hero element. A visual evidence wall that looks like what you see in detective shows: documents pinned to a board, red string connecting related items, timeline running along the bottom, photos, maps, sticky notes with questions. Interactive — click any element to zoom in.

Investigation Status — Every case has a clear state: queued → under investigation → findings published. The homepage shows progress like a mission tracker. Creates momentum and a reason to come back.

Dual Voice — Human annotations appear differently from AI analysis. Maybe handwritten-style notes for human observations, clean monospace for AI findings. Creates a visible dialogue between the two investigators.

The "What We Found" Moment — Each published case has a clear payoff: "Here's what nobody noticed before." This is the hook that makes people share and come back. It's not just presenting evidence — it's uncovering something.

Living Progress — The site should feel alive. A counter showing "3 of 821 cases investigated." The current active case with a progress indicator. A queue of what's next. People should feel like they're watching an ongoing operation.
