USS Nimitz Encounter

November 14, 2004 · Off San Diego, CA · 31.5°N, 117.5°W · 6+ military witnesses

For two weeks in November 2004, the USS Princeton — part of the Nimitz Carrier Strike Group — tracked anomalous objects on its AN/SPY-1B radar. The objects appeared at altitudes above 80,000 feet before dropping to near sea level in seconds.

Commander David Fravor and Lt. Commander Alex Dietrich were vectored to investigate. Fravor observed a white, oblong object — roughly 40 feet long, shaped like a Tic Tac — hovering and making erratic movements over a disturbance in the ocean. When he descended to engage, the object accelerated beyond visual range in less than two seconds. A second aircraft subsequently captured it on FLIR.

The Pentagon acknowledged the video's authenticity in 2020. Commander Fravor testified under oath before Congress in 2023.
