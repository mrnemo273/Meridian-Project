Query: What cases describe triangular craft with silent propulsion?
High Confidence Sufficient evidence across multiple independent sources Searched: 821 cases + 147K narratives

Triangular craft with silent or near-silent propulsion appear in 37 curated cases across the Meridian corpus, spanning from 1965 to 2021. An additional ~2,400 NUFORC narratives describe triangular objects, though most lack sufficient corroboration for curated case status.

The most concentrated period for triangular craft reports is 1989–1993, driven primarily by the Belgian Wave [Belgian Wave, 1989–1990], which produced the largest cluster of credentialed, radar-corroborated triangular observations in the record. Belgian Air Force F-16 interceptors obtained radar locks on a triangular object that accelerated from 280 to 1,100 knots while dropping from 7,000 to 5,000 feet in approximately one second [Belgian Wave — F-16 Intercepts, 30 Mar 1990].

Of the 37 curated triangular-craft cases, 32 (86%) describe silent propulsion — a rate significantly higher than the corpus-wide average of 23% for silent propulsion across all craft types. This suggests a particularly strong association between triangular geometry and the absence of audible propulsion.

Note: This correlation does not imply causation. The descriptor co-occurrence may reflect observer expectation effects or a genuine physical characteristic.

Notable curated cases describing triangular silent craft include the Belgian Wave [1989–1990, Tier 1], the Illinois Triangle [5 Jan 2000, Tier 2] observed by five police officers across multiple municipalities, the Cosford RAF Incident [30 Mar 1993, Tier 2] where multiple RAF stations reported a silent triangular object, and the Hudson Valley Wave [1982–1986, Tier 2] involving thousands of witnesses across New York and Connecticut.

Geographically, triangular-craft cases show concentrations in Western Europe (particularly the Low Countries), the U.S. Midwest, and the eastern seaboard. There is no statistically significant correlation with military installations, though several cases occur near air force bases [geographic analysis, v2].
